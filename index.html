PLANETS OF THE GALAXY

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun.
It has a very thin atmosphere, which causes extreme temperature fluctuations.
The surface temperature can reach up to 800°F (430°C) during the day and drop to -330°F (-201°C) at night.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun.
Known as Earth's twin due to its similar size, it has a thick atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide.
Thus creating a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in the solar system. The surface temperature averages 900°F (475°C).

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life.
It has a breathable atmosphere, a liquid water surface, and a temperate climate.
Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun.
The surface of Mars is orange-red because it is covered in iron(III) oxide dust, giving it the nickname "the Red Planet".
Mars is among the brightest objects in Earth's sky, and its high-contrast albedo features have made it a common subject for telescope viewing.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in the solar system.
It is a gas giant with a thick atmosphere made primarily of hydrogen and helium.
Jupiter is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is best known for its stunning ring system.
It is a gas giant like Jupiter, with an atmosphere mostly made of hydrogen and helium.
Saturn has at least 82 moons, with Titan being its largest moon.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun.
It is unique for its tilted rotation axis, causing it to spin on its side.
The planet's atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a blue-green color.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun.
It has a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.
Neptune is known for its strong winds and large storms, including the Great Dark Spot, which is similar to Jupiter’s Great Red Spot.
